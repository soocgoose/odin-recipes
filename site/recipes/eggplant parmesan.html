Eggplant Parmesan

Description

This heavy rich recipe is best enjoyed right from the oven and taken in modest amounts. The effort involved isn't too complex but it will take some time. Serve for a group for best results.

Ingredients

Eggplant

  • black pepper (freshly ground)
  • 2 medium eggplants (2-1/2 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 5 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons whole milk
  • 4 cups Italian-style breadcrumbs
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1-1/2 to 2 cups vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan (grated)
  • 1 pound provolone cheese (grated)
  • 1-1/2 pounds mozzarella cheese
  • 2 handfuls of fresh basil (torn)

Sauce

  • kosher salt
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 4 medium yellow onions
  • 1 tablespoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 3 cans (28 ounces) whole plum tomatoes

Steps

Eggplant

  1. Wash and cut the eggplant into 1/2 inch thick rounds.
  2. Arrange the eggplant rounds in a single layer on two baking sheets.
  3. Sprinkle with salt on both sides of each slice and allow to sit for about 1 hour (salting it draws out the liquid and bitter flavor).
  4. After an hour, rinse with cold water and dry them thoroughly with a kitchen towel.
  5. Put the flour in a medium bowl and season with salt and pepper.
  6. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk and season with salt and pepper.
  7. In a third bowl, combine the breadcrumbs with the oregano and fresh thyme leaves and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Dip each eggplant slice in the flour and shake off any excess.
  9. Then, dip in the egg mixture, and finally in the breadcrumbs (make sure to coat both sides of each slice of eggplant).
  10. Arrange them in single layers on the baking sheets.
  11. In a large skillet, pour enough oil to accumulate about 1/2 inch in the bottom.
  12. Heat the oil until it begins to smoke lightly (alternatively, test with a thermometer and wait until the oil registers between 380⁰ F and 400⁰ F).
  13. Use a pair of kitchen tongs to add a single layer of the eggplant to the pan.
  14. Cook them until they are golden brown, about 2 minutes on each side.
  15. Remove from the oil and transfer to a baking sheet fitted with paper towel so the eggplant can drain as the others cook (alternatively, use a cooling rack).
  16. Season lightly with salt.
  17. Take care to reheat the oil back up to temperature before adding another batch of slices to the pan.

Sauce

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Peel, halve, and cut the onion into thin slices.
  3. Peel and grate the garlic cloves.
  4. Add the onion and garlic, season with salt and red pepper flakes.
  5. Cook until the onions become translucent, 3 to 5 minutes.
  6. Add the sugar and the canned tomatoes.
  7. Use a wooden spoon to break up some of the whole tomatoes and cook 10 to 15 minutes over medium heat, stirring from time to time.
  8. Taste for seasoning, the tomatoes should be fairly broken down and the flavors coming together.
  9. Cook for another few minutes if the tomatoes still taste like they need a little more time to break down.
  10. Set aside to cool.

Eggplant and Sauce

  1. Cut the mozzarella into thin slices.
  2. Preheat oven to 350⁰ F.
  3. In a 9 by 13 inch flameproof baking dish, spoon about 1/4 of the tomato sauce on the bottom.
  4. Top with a layer of the fried eggplant (the eggplant slices can overlap slightly).
  5. Top with about 1/3 of the mozzarella slices.
  6. Sprinkle with about 1/4 of the Parmesan and provolone cheeses.
  7. Top with a layer of torn basil leaves.
  8. Spoon sauce and repeat the layering two more times to make three layers.
  9. End with the remaining mozzarella.
  10. Carefully press the layers down firmly into the dish once assembled.
  11. Place the dish on the top rack of the oven and cook until the cheese is melted and bubbly (35 to 40 minutes).
  12. For extra browning, put the dish under the broiler for a minute or two just before serving to get an extra brown cheesy top.
